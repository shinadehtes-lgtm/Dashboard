M&A Strategy — Board Dialogue Summary
M&A: What the Board Established

The board had an extended discussion on M&A methodology. The following represents the framework and open questions that emerged — relevant context for S&P given the partnership implications of acquisition targets.

1

Three acquisition categories, different readiness levels

Operating assets and development pipelines are well within existing IC capability. Full IPP / operating hold-co acquisitions are new territory — independent analysis is being brought in specifically for these. The board signalled it expects to be closely involved in evaluating the latter category.

2

Key evaluation criteria confirmed

Accretiveness to sustainable revenue target (currently projected 2029). Skill set enhancement vs. organic build cost. Integration behavioural and cultural risk — flagged as the hardest element, not financial. Country risk lens for cross-border targets (Namibia, Zimbabwe, Zambia, West Africa all in active pipeline).

3

Funding structure: ring-fencing vs. group balance sheet

CFO confirmed the team is evaluating pre-funding products currently available in the market. The decision on ring-fencing vs. group balance sheet treatment will be asset-specific. PRC → IC → Board loop is being tightened so board ratification comes with full context, not cold.

4

The "non-participation risk" was explicitly named

Market consolidation is accelerating. The board was clear that the risk of not participating in M&A is as material as the risk of overpaying. Gareth (Nedbank NED) drew a direct parallel to banking consolidation in Africa — and noted that Nedbank had paid a significant premium for a Kenyan acquisition because the market had moved and they had to act. The lesson: set hurdle rates and country risk frameworks before deal opportunities arrive, not during.

Immediate Priorities

What Strategy & Partnerships Needs to Act On

1

Develop the Global Citizen strategic framework for board ratification

The 10GW / 10-year commitment exists publicly. The board needs a formal strategy paper that translates this commitment into a structured plan with milestones, funding logic, and partnership architecture. This is the most urgent governance deliverable for the S&P function.

2

Formalise the partnership matrix review and bring outcomes to the board

The review has been running for 18 months. With Umoya closed and the market positioning shift confirmed, it is time to present the revised partner segmentation, criteria, and forward partnership principles to the board for endorsement.

3

Establish Norfund new CEO engagement strategy

Relationship continuity with a transitioning institutional partner is a known vulnerability. An early-engagement plan for the new Norfund CEO should be activated without delay.

4

Support M&A with partnership diligence and integration frameworks

The CEO and board named cultural integration as the hardest part of M&A. S&P can contribute the partner diligence lens — assessing whether acquisition targets share the Pele fabric and values — before commercial processes accelerate.

5

Own the narrative discipline as profile scales

The CEO explicitly named the risk that a strong tailwind can let others drive the Pele story. The Crisis Communications Plan, the three-tier public engagement framework, and the shift to "Human Stories" storytelling must all be operationalised this year — not aspirational.
